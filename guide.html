01

What the app does

The Scotland Overland Reel Maker lets you combine your real customer reviews with your own Land Rover photography to create professional-looking Instagram Reel frames — ready to post in seconds, with no design software needed.

Each Reel is exported as a full 1080 × 1920px PNG image (the correct size for Instagram Reels and Stories). You choose the photo, pick a review, adjust the styling, and save.

📋
Import reviews

Load reviews from the Google scraper CSV, paste them manually, or bulk-paste multiple at once.

🖼
Upload photos

Add as many Land Rover photos as you like and switch between them with a click.

✏️
Edit everything

Shorten review text, correct names, adjust stars — all editable before saving.

🎨
Style controls

Adjust overlay darkness, text size, quote position, website URL size, and overlay colour.

🔍
Reposition image

Drag the photo to frame your shot perfectly, zoom in, and nudge with arrow buttons.

💾
Save instantly

Generate the Reel image and right-click to save — no downloads, no accounts, no cost.

02

Getting started

Open the app in any modern browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge). No login or installation is required. The app runs entirely in your browser — nothing is uploaded to any server.

The screen is split into two columns. The left column is where you manage reviews and photos. The right column is where you style and preview your Reel.

💡 Good to know The app uses no AI tokens and requires no Claude account. It works completely offline once the page has loaded — your photos and review text never leave your computer.
03

Importing your reviews

There are three ways to get reviews into the app. Choose the one that suits your situation.

Option A — CSV file (recommended)

If you use the GBP Review Scraper Chrome extension, it exports a .csv file with all your Google reviews including names, star ratings, dates and full text.

1
Run the extension on your Google Business listing

Navigate to your Google Maps or Google Search business page and activate the extension. It will scroll, expand, and extract all reviews automatically.

2
Save the exported CSV file

The extension will download a file named something like gbp_reviews_xxx.csv. Save it somewhere you can find it.

3
Click the CSV file tab in the app

In the "Add reviews" section, click the CSV file tab, then click the upload area and select your file.

4
Review the preview and remove any you don't want

Each review appears in a list. Click × next to any you want to remove before adding. Blank reviews are skipped automatically.

5
Click "Add X reviews to list"

All remaining reviews are loaded into your queue, ready to use.

Option B — Single paste

For adding one review at a time. Click the Single tab, paste any review text into the box. The app will try to detect the reviewer name and star rating automatically. You can correct them manually before clicking Add to list.

This also works if you paste a single CSV-formatted row directly from the extension file, for example: "Rob","Rated 5.0 out of 5","a month ago","Great experience!",""

Option C — Bulk paste

Click the Bulk paste tab and paste multiple reviews at once. The parser will attempt to split them into individual entries. Click Parse reviews to see the results, remove any you don't want, then click Add all to list.

💡 Bulk paste works best when Reviews are separated by blank lines, or pasted directly from the CSV file (one row per line). You can also mix and match — import a CSV file first, then add extra ones manually.
04

Managing the review list

Once reviews are in the queue they appear in the Reviews panel on the left. The badge shows the total count.

→
Select a review for the preview

Click the review text to select it. It highlights in green and appears in the preview on the right.

→
Edit the reviewer name

Click directly on the name (shown in grey next to the stars) and type to change it. The preview updates instantly.

→
Edit or shorten the review text

Click the ✎ pencil icon to expand a full text editor. Type to shorten or reword the review — the preview updates as you type. Click ✎ again to close it.

→
Delete a review

Click the × button on the right of any review to remove it from the list permanently.

⚠ Keep review text concise Long reviews wrap across many lines and can dominate the image. Aim for 100–180 characters — one or two punchy sentences work best on a Reel frame.
05

Adding your photos

Click the upload area in the Photos panel and select your Land Rover images. You can select multiple files at once — they all appear as thumbnails below the upload zone.

Click any thumbnail to switch to that photo in the preview. To remove a photo, hover over its thumbnail and click the red × that appears in the corner.

💡 Best photos for Reels Landscape-orientation photos with sky, open terrain, or dramatic scenery work best — there's more space around the subject for the text overlay to sit clearly. The app fills the full 9:16 frame by cropping to fit.
06

Styling your Reel

The Style panel on the right gives you control over how the text overlay looks.

→
Quote position — Bottom / Centre / Top

Controls where on the image the review block sits. Bottom is the Instagram-standard position; centre works well for symmetrical shots.

→
Text nudge ↕

Fine-tunes the vertical position of the text block. Drag right to move it down, left to move it up — useful when the main position (bottom/centre/top) puts the text over an important part of your photo.

→
Overlay darkness

Controls how dark the semi-transparent gradient behind the text is. Increase it if your text is hard to read against a bright background.

→
Text size

Sets the size of the review quote text. Shorter reviews can afford a larger size; longer reviews may need to be smaller to fit.

→
Website text size

Controls the size of the scotlandoverland.com URL shown at the bottom of the text block, independently of the review text size.

→
Overlay colour

Choose between Dark/black, Forest green, or Slate grey for the gradient behind the text. Forest green ties nicely to the Scotland Overland brand.

→
Show branding

Toggles the "SCOTLAND OVERLAND" and website URL at the bottom of the text block. Leave this on for branded content.

07

Positioning the image

Below the preview is an Image position & zoom section that lets you frame your shot exactly as you want it.

→
Drag to pan

Click and drag directly on the preview to move the photo around within the frame. The text overlay stays fixed; only the photo moves.

→
Arrow buttons

The ↑ ↓ ← → buttons nudge the photo in precise steps — useful for small adjustments.

→
Zoom slider

Drag right to zoom into the photo. Zooming in gives you more room to pan and lets you crop out unwanted edges.

→
Scroll wheel

Hovering over the preview and scrolling also zooms in and out.

→
Reset button

Click Reset to return the photo to its default centred position and zoom level.

08

Generating and saving your Reel

When you're happy with the preview, click the green Generate save image button. The finished Reel appears below the button at full resolution.

1
Right-click the generated image

A context menu appears with save options.

2
Click "Save image as…"

Choose where to save it on your computer. The file is a full-quality 1080 × 1920px PNG.

3
Alternatively, drag it to your desktop

Click and hold the image, then drag it straight to your desktop or a folder — it saves as a PNG file instantly.

⚠ If "Save image as…" doesn't appear Some browsers show "Copy image" instead. This copies the full-resolution image to your clipboard and you can paste it directly into Instagram, WhatsApp, or your photos app. Alternatively try a different browser — Chrome and Firefox both reliably show "Save image as…".
💡 Uploading to Instagram In the Instagram app, tap the + button, select the saved PNG from your camera roll or downloads, choose Reel as the format, and post as a static image Reel or add music and transitions in the editor.
09

Recommended workflow

Here's the most efficient way to produce a batch of Reels in one sitting:

1
Run the scraper extension and download your CSV

Do this once and keep the CSV file saved. You can reload it any time to refresh your review pool.

2
Import the CSV and tidy up your reviews

Remove any non-English reviews or very short entries. Use the ✎ editor to shorten long reviews down to a punchy 1–2 sentences.

3
Upload a batch of your best photos

Aim for variety — wide landscape shots, vehicle detail shots, camping scenes. More photos gives you more combinations to work with.

4
Set your style once and keep it consistent

Choose your overlay colour, darkness, and text size, then leave them the same for all your Reels so your feed looks cohesive.

5
Work through photo + review combinations

Select a review, switch to a photo, pan/zoom to frame it, generate and save. Repeat for each combination.

10

Tips for great Reels

📏 Keep review text short 100–180 characters is the sweet spot. One memorable sentence performs better on a Reel than a full paragraph. Use the ✎ editor to trim longer reviews down to their best line.
🌅 Match the mood of the photo to the review A dramatic Highland landscape pairs well with an adventure-focused quote. A cosy campsite photo pairs well with a comfort-focused quote. Think about what the image conveys before you pick the review.
🎨 Use Forest green overlay for branded content The dark green overlay ties the Reel to your Scotland Overland brand colours and feels more distinctive than a plain black overlay.
📸 Portrait photos work best The Reel frame is 9:16 (taller than wide). Portrait-orientation or square photos fill the frame naturally. Landscape photos work too — zoom in slightly and pan to the most interesting area.
🔄 Refresh your reviews regularly Every few months, re-run the scraper extension to pick up new Google reviews and import a fresh CSV. New reviews from recent customers are the most compelling social proof.
🗂 Save your Reels in a dedicated folder Keep a folder called "Reels ready to post" so you always have a queue ready to go and never need to scramble for content.
